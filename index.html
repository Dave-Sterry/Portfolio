About Me

A long time Oregonian, I currently live in NE Portland with my wife Jean and our adorable dog Helo. A long time runner, mountain biker, climber, and recovering cycle-cross racer I try to spend as much of my free time outside as I can. If I’m not inside watching movies, playing videos games or practicing guitar, you’ll probably find me outside on an adventure, or just wandering around my neighborhood with Jean and Helo.

Background

Originally from the East Coast I moved to Portland with my family when I was 10 years old. Hard to believe that was 27 years ago now! After high school I went to Southern Oregon University and got a BS in Sociology. A year after I finished my degree I went to teach English in Japan for a year in the medium sized town of Saijo. Even though my Japanese language skills were mediocre at best, I still had a wonderful experience. After returning to Portland in late 2007, I got a job at a small law firm as an office assistant. After 4 years in that position I was promoted to IT manager for the firm. This mostly meant I did onsite desktop support for all the staff, but I learned quite a bit extra with the help of our IT consultant firm while in the position. After staying in that position for 6 years I decided it was time to move on and try something new, and left in late 2018 to pursue a career in Physical Therapy. I spent a year and a half taking general science classes at PCC in preparation to apply to a PT grad program. However once COVID hit the US it became near impossible to get the required observation hours I needed to apply to the program. So I turned to another career path that had interested me for years. Programming.

Why Programming?

So why programming? The simplest answer is that I like to understand how things work, and then use that knowledge to build something and since I was young I’ve loved messing around on computers. I’m old enough to remember having to launch all my PC games out of the command line, something that felt like magic when I was 10. The more complicated answer is that I also recognize that this is a very in demand skill set, and is only becoming more so. And while I may have many interests outside of coding, I know learning to program will let me pursue a career at many different companies. Ideally one that makes or supports one of my interests.
